Pregnancy & Pediatric Chiropractic

Gentle, safe chiropractic care for expecting mothers, newborns, and growing children in Fredericksburg, VA.

Prenatal Chiropractic Care

Pregnancy transforms your body in extraordinary ways — and those changes can bring real discomfort. As your baby grows, your center of gravity shifts, your pelvis widens, and your spine adapts to carry increasing weight. These shifts often lead to lower back pain, hip pain, round ligament pain, and sciatica that can make the journey feel harder than it needs to be.

Chiropractic care during pregnancy is a safe, gentle, and drug-free way to address these issues. At Milestone, Dr. Meggen Haselhorst specializes in prenatal chiropractic and is certified in the Webster Technique — a specific chiropractic analysis and adjustment designed to reduce sacral subluxation and improve pelvic function during pregnancy.

Benefits by Trimester

First Trimester

Early pregnancy brings hormonal changes that begin loosening your ligaments. Chiropractic care during this stage helps maintain proper spinal alignment as your body prepares for the months ahead. Many patients also report that regular adjustments help reduce nausea and fatigue.

Second Trimester

As your belly grows and your posture shifts, lower back pain and hip discomfort often begin. Adjustments during the second trimester focus on maintaining pelvic balance, reducing muscle tension, and keeping you comfortable as your body changes more noticeably.

Third Trimester

The third trimester is when chiropractic care often provides the most dramatic relief. Pelvic alignment becomes especially important for creating optimal space for the baby to move into the best position for delivery. The Webster Technique is most commonly applied during this stage to address pelvic imbalance and support a more comfortable labor and delivery.

The Webster Technique

The Webster Technique is a specific sacral analysis and chiropractic adjustment used throughout pregnancy. It works by reducing sacral subluxation, which in turn helps balance the muscles and ligaments of the pelvis. When the pelvis is properly aligned, the uterus has more room, potentially allowing the baby to assume the optimal head-down position for birth.

The International Chiropractic Pediatric Association (ICPA) reports a high rate of success with this technique. It is gentle, safe, and widely recommended by midwives and OB-GYNs as a complement to standard prenatal care.

What Expecting Mothers Can Expect

  • A thorough initial evaluation of your spinal and pelvic alignment
  • Specialized pregnancy pillows and adjustable tables for your comfort
  • Gentle, low-force adjustments — no twisting, no cracking
  • Treatment plans coordinated with your OB-GYN or midwife
  • Guidance on stretches and exercises safe for each trimester
  • A supportive, judgment-free environment

Pediatric Chiropractic Care

Chiropractic care is not just for adults. Children — from newborns to teenagers — can benefit from gentle spinal assessments and adjustments that support healthy development and address common childhood conditions.

Why Children Need Chiropractic Care

The birth process itself can place significant stress on an infant's spine and nervous system. As children grow, they experience falls, sports impacts, heavy backpacks, and hours of screen time — all of which can contribute to spinal misalignment. Pediatric chiropractic care addresses these issues early, before they develop into chronic problems.

Conditions We Help With

  • Colic and excessive crying in infants
  • Difficulty with breastfeeding or latching
  • Ear infections and fluid buildup
  • Growing pains
  • Poor posture and "tech neck" in older children
  • Sports injuries in young athletes
  • Bedwetting
  • General wellness and immune support

Is It Safe?

Absolutely. Pediatric chiropractic adjustments use extremely light pressure — often no more than the amount of force you would use to test the ripeness of a tomato. Our doctors are specifically trained in pediatric techniques and adapt their approach based on the child's age, size, and comfort level. The treatment is painless, and most children enjoy their visits.
