Understanding Back Pain & Sciatica

Lower back pain is the single leading cause of disability worldwide, and sciatica — the sharp, radiating pain that travels from the lower back down through the buttock and leg — affects up to 40% of people at some point in their lives. If you are dealing with either condition, you know how much it can disrupt your daily routine, your sleep, and your quality of life.

At Milestone Chiropractic Centre, we see back pain and sciatica patients every day. Our approach is straightforward: find the root cause, treat it directly, and give you the tools to prevent it from coming back.

Common Symptoms We Treat

  • Dull, aching pain in the lower back that worsens with sitting or standing
  • Sharp, shooting pain radiating down one or both legs
  • Numbness or tingling in the legs, feet, or toes
  • Muscle weakness in the lower extremities
  • Stiffness and reduced range of motion in the lower back
  • Pain that intensifies after prolonged sitting, bending, or lifting
  • Difficulty standing up straight or walking comfortably

What Causes Back Pain and Sciatica?

Back pain and sciatica can result from a wide range of underlying issues. The most common causes we see in our Fredericksburg practice include:

  • Herniated or bulging discs — When the soft cushion between your vertebrae pushes out of position, it can press on nearby nerves
  • Spinal misalignment (subluxation) — Vertebrae that have shifted out of their proper position create nerve interference and muscle tension
  • Degenerative disc disease — Age-related wear and tear on the spinal discs can lead to chronic pain and stiffness
  • Piriformis syndrome — The piriformis muscle in the buttock can tighten and compress the sciatic nerve
  • Spinal stenosis — Narrowing of the spinal canal puts pressure on the nerves
  • Poor posture and ergonomics — Daily habits like slouching at a desk or sleeping in awkward positions contribute to chronic strain

How Chiropractic Care Helps

Chiropractic treatment for back pain and sciatica works by addressing the mechanical causes of your pain rather than simply masking symptoms. Here is what our treatment approach typically includes:

  • Spinal adjustments — Precise, controlled force applied to misaligned vertebrae to restore proper position and relieve nerve pressure
  • Flexion-distraction technique — A gentle, non-thrusting approach ideal for disc-related conditions that decompresses the spine
  • Soft tissue therapy — Targeted work on muscles and connective tissue to release tension and improve flexibility
  • Rehabilitation exercises — Custom stretching and strengthening programs you can do at home to support your recovery
  • Ergonomic and lifestyle guidance — Practical advice on posture, sleeping positions, and daily habits to prevent recurrence
