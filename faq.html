🏁 Getting Started

Your first visit at Milestone Chiropractic begins with a thorough consultation where we review your health history and listen to your concerns. Next, we perform a postural assessment, range-of-motion testing, and orthopedic and neurological evaluation. If clinically indicated, we take digital X-rays to get a clear picture of your spinal alignment.

After the evaluation, your doctor will explain their findings in plain language and recommend a personalized care plan. You will always know exactly what we found and why we are recommending specific treatment before any adjustment takes place.

Plan for about 45–60 minutes for your initial visit. This allows enough time for the full examination, X-rays if needed, and a detailed review of findings with your doctor. Follow-up visits are typically 15–30 minutes.

No referral is needed to see a chiropractor in Virginia. Chiropractic doctors are primary care providers for musculoskeletal conditions, and you can schedule directly with our office. Some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage purposes — call us and we can help you verify your benefits before your visit.

Please bring:

  • A photo ID and insurance card
  • A list of any current medications or supplements
  • Any relevant imaging (X-rays, MRI, CT scans) if you have them
  • Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing if possible

You can also complete your new patient paperwork online before your visit to save time. Visit the New Patient Center →

🦴 The Adjustment

Most patients feel immediate relief after an adjustment. Some experience mild soreness for 24–48 hours — similar to how your muscles feel after a good workout. This is a normal response as your body adapts to its corrected alignment.

Our doctors use a range of techniques and always work within your comfort level. If you are apprehensive or have had a bad experience elsewhere, let us know — we have gentle, low-force options for every patient.

The sound is called a “cavitation” — it is simply gas (carbon dioxide and nitrogen) releasing from the fluid inside your spinal joints when the joint is gently moved through its range of motion. It is the same mechanism as when you crack your knuckles. The sound is not bones cracking, and it has nothing to do with the effectiveness of the adjustment. Many effective adjustments produce no sound at all.

Our doctors are trained in multiple techniques and select the most appropriate approach based on your condition, age, and preferences:

  • Diversified — the most common hands-on manual adjustment
  • Thompson Drop Technique — uses a specialized table for enhanced precision
  • Activator Method — a gentle, handheld instrument delivers a low-force impulse
  • Flexion-Distraction — gentle disc decompression, excellent for sciatica and disc herniations
  • Gonstead — highly specific analysis and adjustment of individual spinal segments

✅ Safety & Effectiveness

Yes. Chiropractic care is one of the safest forms of healthcare available for musculoskeletal conditions. The risk of serious adverse events from chiropractic adjustment is extremely low — far lower than the risks associated with long-term NSAID use or spinal surgery.

Our doctors perform a comprehensive evaluation before recommending any treatment. If your condition is better suited to a different type of care, we will tell you and refer you appropriately.

Yes, and the evidence base has grown significantly over the past two decades. A 2017 systematic review published in JAMA found that spinal manipulation produced statistically significant improvements in both pain and function for acute lower back pain. The American College of Physicians now recommends spinal manipulation as a first-line treatment for both acute and chronic low back pain.

Chiropractic care is also supported by research for neck pain, headaches, and certain types of joint dysfunction. We are happy to share specific studies with curious patients — just ask.

👶 Pregnancy & Children

Yes — when performed by a trained chiropractor, prenatal chiropractic care is safe throughout all three trimesters. Our doctors use specialized techniques and tables designed for pregnant patients. We never apply pressure to the abdomen, and all techniques are adapted to your stage of pregnancy.

Research supports chiropractic care for relieving low back pain, pelvic girdle pain, and round ligament pain during pregnancy. We also utilize the Webster Technique, which is specifically designed to optimize pelvic alignment and support optimal fetal positioning. Learn more →

Yes. Pediatric chiropractic care is safe and effective. Adjustments for children use very gentle, fingertip pressure — nothing like the adjustments performed on adults. Many parents bring their children for chiropractic care to address conditions like colic, ear infections, torticollis, scoliosis screening, sports injuries, and postural issues from heavy backpack use or excessive screen time.

Children often respond quickly to chiropractic care because their bodies are still developing and adapting. Learn about our pediatric services →

The Webster Technique is a specific chiropractic analysis and adjustment protocol designed for pregnant patients. It focuses on balancing the pelvis and reducing tension in the uterine ligaments, which may create more space for the baby to move into the optimal head-down position for delivery.

It is a gentle, non-invasive technique performed entirely from the outside. Many OB/GYNs and midwives now refer pregnant patients for Webster Technique care. Read more about our prenatal chiropractic services →

🩺 Conditions We Treat

Yes. Many headaches — including tension headaches and cervicogenic headaches (those originating from the neck) — respond very well to chiropractic care. Misalignment of the cervical vertebrae can cause muscle tension and nerve irritation that triggers headache patterns. Multiple clinical studies have found that spinal manipulation reduces both the frequency and intensity of certain headache types.

Absolutely. Sciatica — pain that radiates from the lower back down through the buttock and leg — is one of the most common conditions we treat. The goal of chiropractic care for sciatica is to identify and correct the underlying cause: typically a disc herniation, vertebral misalignment, or piriformis muscle tension that is compressing the sciatic nerve.

Flexion-distraction therapy and specific lumbar adjustments are particularly effective for sciatic nerve pain. Learn more about our back pain and sciatica care →

In many cases, yes. Non-surgical chiropractic care — particularly flexion-distraction therapy — has been shown to reduce disc herniation symptoms by creating gentle negative pressure within the disc and reducing pressure on affected nerve roots. The American College of Physicians recommends trying conservative care, including spinal manipulation, before considering surgery for disc-related back pain.
