How Chiropractic Adjustments Alleviate Lower Back Pain

Dr. Sarah Mitchell, D.C.
Milestone Chiropractic Centre
April 10, 2026 8 min read

Lower back pain is the leading cause of disability worldwide, affecting approximately 80% of Americans at some point in their lives. Whether it comes from a workplace injury, a car accident, poor posture, or simply years of wear and tear, back pain can derail your daily life, limit your mobility, and make even simple activities feel impossible.

The good news? Chiropractic care has a decades-long track record of helping patients find lasting relief — without surgery, opioids, or lengthy recovery times. Here is what the science says, and what you can expect from care at Milestone Chiropractic Centre.

Why Lower Back Pain Is So Common

The lumbar spine (your lower back) bears the majority of your body weight and handles the most mechanical stress during daily activities. Five lumbar vertebrae, along with the discs between them, form a complex structure that supports movement in virtually every direction.

When any component of this system — a disc, a joint, a nerve, or the surrounding muscles — becomes misaligned, compressed, or irritated, the result is pain. Common causes include:

  • Vertebral subluxation — misalignment of spinal bones that irritates surrounding nerves
  • Disc herniation or bulge — when disc material presses on a nearby nerve root
  • Muscle strain or spasm — from overuse, injury, or compensating for an underlying misalignment
  • Sciatica — irritation of the sciatic nerve causing pain that radiates down the leg
  • Degenerative disc disease — gradual breakdown of disc tissue with age
  • Facet joint dysfunction — arthritis or inflammation in the small joints of the spine

How Chiropractic Adjustments Work

A chiropractic adjustment (also called spinal manipulation) is a controlled, targeted force applied to a specific spinal joint. The goal is to restore proper alignment, improve range of motion, and remove interference from the nervous system.

At Milestone Chiropractic, our doctors use several adjustment techniques depending on your condition, your age, and your comfort level:

  • Diversified Technique — the most common hands-on adjustment, delivering a quick, precise thrust to restore joint mobility
  • Thompson Drop Technique — uses a specialized table with drop mechanisms to enhance gentle adjustments
  • Activator Method — a small handheld instrument delivers a precise, low-force impulse, ideal for sensitive patients
  • Flexion-Distraction — a gentle, pumping motion that decompresses spinal discs, especially effective for herniated discs and sciatica

What Does the Research Show?

Chiropractic care for lower back pain is not just anecdotal — it is supported by a growing body of clinical evidence. A 2017 systematic review published in JAMA found that spinal manipulation was associated with statistically significant improvements in pain and function for patients with acute lower back pain. The American College of Physicians now recommends spinal manipulation as a first-line treatment for both acute and chronic low back pain before considering medications.

A landmark study by the Journal of the American Medical Association found that chiropractic care produced better outcomes than medical care alone for a significant percentage of low back pain cases — and did so at a lower overall cost.

What to Expect During Your Visit

Your first appointment at Milestone Chiropractic begins with a thorough consultation and examination. We will review your health history, assess your posture and range of motion, and may take X-rays to get a clear picture of your spinal alignment. Based on this evaluation, your doctor will develop a personalized care plan.

Most patients begin to feel improvement within the first few visits. A typical care plan for lower back pain includes:

  • Phase 1 (Relief) — more frequent visits to reduce pain and inflammation, typically 2-3 times per week for 4-6 weeks
  • Phase 2 (Correction) — less frequent visits as your spine stabilizes and heals
  • Phase 3 (Maintenance) — periodic wellness visits to maintain alignment and prevent recurrence

Complementary Approaches We Recommend

Chiropractic adjustments work best as part of a comprehensive approach to back health. Our doctors may also recommend:

  • Targeted stretching and core strengthening exercises you can do at home
  • Ergonomic adjustments for your workspace
  • Ice and heat therapy protocols
  • Nutritional support to reduce systemic inflammation
  • Massage therapy to address muscle tension alongside spinal adjustments

Is Chiropractic Care Right for You?

Chiropractic care is safe and effective for the vast majority of patients with lower back pain. Our doctors perform a thorough evaluation before recommending any treatment, and we will always be transparent about whether chiropractic care is the right fit for your specific condition.

If you have been living with back pain, do not wait for it to become a bigger problem. Most patients are surprised by how quickly they begin to feel better once proper alignment is restored.
